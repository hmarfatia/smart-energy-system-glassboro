# Smart Energy System for Glassboro, NJ

## Overview
This project consists of designing a smart energy system for the town of Glassboro, NJ using IoT sensors and AWS cloud services to remotely monitor and optimize energy usage. Analysis of sector-based consumption rates identifed high-demand areas where scalable microgrid solutions can be integrated to improve factors like efficiency and cost.

## Problem
Modern cities struggle to implement an efficient grid infrastructure, leading to overwhelming demand and limited integration of renewable energy sources. The town of Glassboro is an example as similar challenges are faced across sectors like residential and commercial areas.

## Objective
To design and develop a smart microgrid system that uses IoT sensors and cloud technology to remotely monitor energy consumption rates, identify areas of high-demand, and optimize energy distribution.

## Methodology
* Analyzing of energy consumption rates across varying sectors (residential, commercial, industrial, transportation, infrastructure)
* Identifiying of energy "hotspots" using data-driven analysis
* Designing an IoT-based monitoring system using smart meters and sensors
* Integrating of cloud architecture (AWS IoT, Lambda)
* Enabling real-time data processing and notification system through MQTT messaging

## Results
* Identified sectors with high consumption and usage patterns
* Introduced strategies for optimal energy distribution
* Demonstrated potential for greater efficiency and reduced energy waste

## Impact
This project provided the framework for a smart city energy system, lowering costs and energy waste as well as supporting the development of sustainable infrastructure.

## Future Work
* Improve device connectivity and security with edge computing and encryption methods
* Integrate AI-based modeling for demand forecasting
